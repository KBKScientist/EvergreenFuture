### Tax Optimization ğŸ¯
The tool automatically:
- Withdraws from taxable accounts first (most tax efficient)
- Then traditional IRAs/401ks
- Then Roth (preserve these!)
- Then HSAs (best for last)

In tax bomb years (student loan forgiveness), it switches to Roth first to avoid stacking taxable income. Smart!

### RMDs (Age 73+) ğŸ”’
Tool enforces Required Minimum Distributions from traditional accounts. You MUST withdraw starting at 73 whether you need money or not.

**Strategy:** Spend down traditional accounts before 73 to avoid forced large withdrawals in high tax brackets.

### Liquid vs Total Net Worth ğŸ’§
- **Liquid** = spendable money (investment accounts)
- **Total** = liquid + home equity
- **Focus on liquid** - you can't eat your house

## ğŸ“ Pro Tips

1. **Be conservative:** Overestimate expenses, underestimate returns. Rather be pleasantly surprised than broke.

2. **Run Monte Carlo:** If your plan barely works in smooth projections, it'll fail in real life. Need buffer!

3. **Use scenarios for everything:** Don't just wonder "what if?" - model it and know the answer!

4. **Update annually:** Come back each year, update balances, adjust assumptions. Track actual vs projected.

5. **Healthcare is expensive:** Use 5% inflation for healthcare costs, not 3%. It grows faster.

6. **Social Security:** If unsure, leave it out. Bonus if you get it!

7. **Save the current plan as a scenario FIRST** before trying wild experiments. You can always go back.

8. **Inflation matters:** $60k today â‰  $60k in 30 years. That's why everything inflates in the tool.

## ğŸ†˜ Common Issues

### "I ran out of money at age 78!"
**Fixes:**
- Increase savings rate now (even 5% helps!)
- Reduce retirement expenses 10-20%

- Run Monte Carlo - might just be unlucky simulation

### "Numbers seem wrong"
- Check income/expense frequency (monthly vs annual)
- Verify account balances are current
- Look for duplicate entries
- Check tax settings (single vs married)
